Personal Projects

Manga-Tagger

NEXT

Background and inspirations

Since I learned about system administrations, I felt in love with selfhosting multiple services on Linux. I then tried to selfhost almost everything I can. I learnt a lot and started to go with Docker which really really helped to ease things up for a simple home-server.

Like the original author, I too really enjoy Japanese culture but I also love Korean (manhwa) and Chinese (manhua) comics and I too, was disappointed by the lack of support for these medias. While the original Manga-Tagger managed to support Japanese comics, it still lacked support for Manhwa and Manhua. It also required FMD2 which also meant it was only made for Windows.

Selfhosting on Windows is a big no for me so I decided to learn some python and adjust Manga-Tagger to work on Linux. I was also learning Docker so for convenience, I also created a docker image. I then I kept updating it for my own convenience but also because it's fun.

Descriptions

Manga-Tagger was made to automatically watch a folder, recognize a comic chapter by its filename to then grab metadata and embed it to the file.
It only works with cbz files.

This fork doesn't require FMD2. Running MangaTagger.py will make it watch the directory configured in the settings.json.


1/5

Docker-FMD2

NEXT

Background and inspirations

After I "updated" Manga-Tagger for myself, I realized a lots of people might want to use FMD2 on Linux as well since FMD2 can grab files and name them just right for my Manga-Tagger version. Rather than port it myself (which I can't), I decided to dockerize it. I immediately thought of Wine and NoVNC and just did it.

Descriptions

It's a dockerized version of Free Manga Downloader 2 (FMD2). The GUI can be accessed with your favorite browser thanks to noVNC.
To make it work, X11VNC, XVFB (for virtual framebuffer), noVNC and fluxbox (for window management, to maximize FMD2) were used.
